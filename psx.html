Pakistan Stock Exchange Reaches Historic Milestone: KSE-100 Crosses 171,000 Points

Date: December 18, 2025 | By: Market Desk Report

KARACHI - The Pakistan Stock Exchange (PSX) has shattered all previous records, achieving a monumental feat by closing at an all-time high of 171,960.4 points on Thursday, December 18, 2025. The benchmark KSE-100 index witnessed a powerful rally, surging by 1,646.79 points in a single trading session, marking a significant 0.97% gain.

This unprecedented growth signals a robust turnaround for the national economy, fueled by a convergence of positive macroeconomic indicators and a surge in both local and foreign investor confidence.

Market Snapshot

  • Current Index: 171,960.4 Points
  • Day's Change: +1,646.79 Points (+0.97%)
  • Trading Volume: 850 Million Shares
  • Top Performing Sectors: Commercial Banking, Oil & Gas Exploration, Technology.

The Catalyst: Economic Stability

Analysts attribute this historic bull run primarily to the stabilization of the country's external account. The State Bank of Pakistan (SBP) recently confirmed a $100 million current account surplus for the preceding month. This surplus has played a pivotal role in stabilizing the Rupee against the US Dollar, providing much-needed breathing room for the economy.

Furthermore, the successful completion of the review with international financial institutions has unlocked new tranches of funding, significantly boosting foreign exchange reserves which now stand at a 5-year high.

"The market is responding to fundamentals. The $100M surplus isn't just a number; it is a signal to the world that Pakistan's fiscal discipline is yielding results."

Sector-Wise Performance

The rally was broad-based, with green zones seen across almost all major sectors. The banking sector led the charge, driven by strong earnings reports and attractive dividend yields. The energy sector followed closely, reacting positively to new government policies regarding circular debt reduction.

Investor Confidence Returns

Market experts have noted a distinct shift in sentiment. "The fear of default is completely off the table," stated Sarah Ahmed, a senior analyst at Topline Securities. "We are seeing the return of foreign portfolio investment (FPI) after years of outflow. Foreign investors are looking at the 171,000 level not as a ceiling, but as a new base for future growth."

Future Outlook

While the mood at the PSX is celebratory, experts advise cautious optimism. The sustainability of this rally depends on the continuation of fiscal reforms and political stability. However, if the current economic trajectory continues, technical analysts predict the index could test the 175,000 barrier before the year ends.

As the closing bell rang on December 18, 2025, the trading floor erupted in cheers, marking a day that will be etched in the history books of Pakistan's financial markets.
