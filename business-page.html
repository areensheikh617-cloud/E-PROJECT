Pakistan Stock Exchange Hits Record High Amid Economic Revival

The Pakistan Stock Exchange (PSX) closed at an unprecedented 171,960.4 points on December 18, 2025, marking another milestone in the country's economic recovery journey. The KSE-100 index gained 1,646.79 points (0.97%) in a single session, driven by positive economic indicators including a $100 million current account surplus in November 2025.

Financial analysts attribute this surge to improving macroeconomic fundamentals, falling bond yields, and increased investor confidence. The three-year rally has seen the index soar over 300%, making Pakistan one of the best-performing stock markets globally.

Key Drivers: Current account surplus, declining inflation, stable currency, and growing foreign investment interest.
