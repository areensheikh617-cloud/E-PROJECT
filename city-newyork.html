üì° Digital Infrastructure

LinkNYC & Free Wi-Fi

NYC has replaced old phone booths with smart tech kiosks:

1. Gigabit Wi-Fi: Largest and fastest free public Wi-Fi network in the world.
2. Charging Ports: Free USB charging stations on sidewalks.
3. Info Tablets: Touchscreens for maps and city services.
4. 5G Hosting: Kiosks also serve as 5G small cell towers.

OMNY Contactless System

The MTA has fully modernized the famous NYC Subway:

1. Tap-and-Go: Use phone, watch, or card to pay for subways and buses.
2. MetroCard Retired: Phasing out plastic cards for digital wallets.

4. CBTC Signaling: Computer-controlled trains for faster frequency.

PropTech & Smart Skyscrapers

NYC is the world leader in Property Technology (PropTech):

1. Smart Energy: AI manages heating/cooling in the Empire State Building.

3. Vertical Living: Tech-enabled luxury apartments with automated services.

Activities & Events in New York

Statue of Liberty

Take a ferry to see the Statue of Liberty and explore the history of immigration at Ellis Island.

Central Park

Walk through the massive park, visit the Zoo, rent a boat, or relax at Sheep Meadow.

Broadway Shows

Watch world-famous musicals and plays in the historic theaters around Times Square.

Times Square

Experience the bright lights, massive billboards, street performers, and midnight energy.

Empire State Building

Go to the observation deck on the 86th or 102nd floor for iconic skyline views of Manhattan.

The MET & MoMA

Visit the Metropolitan Museum of Art (The MET) and the Museum of Modern Art for world-class art.

Brooklyn Bridge

Walk across the historic Brooklyn Bridge for stunning views of the skyline and East River.

Shopping on 5th Ave

Window shop at luxury brands like Tiffany & Co, Saks, and Apple Store on Fifth Avenue.

The High Line

Walk along this unique public park built on a historic freight rail line elevated above the streets.

Sports Events

Watch a Yankees baseball game in the Bronx or a Knicks basketball game at Madison Square Garden.

NYC Food Scene

Try the famous $1 pizza slices, New York bagels with cream cheese, and street food carts.
